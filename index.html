Manifesto for Agile Business

We have found that the Agile Manifesto applies to more than just software development. Through this work we have rewritten the Agile Manifest to better suit the business as a whole.

Individuals and interactions over processes and tools

Delivering business outcomes over comprehenisive documentation

Customer collaboration over contract negotiation

Responding to change over following a plan

While the right hand side is important, we value the items on left more.

We follow these principles..

  1. Our highest priority is to satisfy the customer through early and continuous delivery of valuable business outcomes.
  2. Welcome changing requirements, even late in delivery. Agile processes harness change for the customer's competitive advantage.
  3. Deliver buesiness outcomes frequently, from a couple of days to a couple of weeks, with a preference to the shorter timescale.
  4. Business and technical people must work together daily.
  5. Build products and teams around motivated individuals. Give them the environment and support they need, and trust them to get the job done.
  6. The most efficient and effective method of conveying information to and within a delivery team is face-to-face conversation.
  7. Business outcomes and customer satisfaction are the primary measures of progress.
  8. Agile processes promote sustainable delivery. The sponsors, delivery team, and users should be able to maintain a constant pace indefinitely.
  9. Continuous attention to technical excellence and good design enhances agility.
  10. Simplicity--the art of maximizing the amount of work not done--is essential.
  11. The best architectures, requirements, and designs emerge from self-organizing teams.
  12. At regular intervals, the team reflects on how to become more effective, then tunes and adjusts its behavior accordingly.
